OCT, or optical coherence tomography, is a non-invasive imaging technique that creates cross-sectional images of the retina and is widely used for diagnosing and monitoring ophthalmic disease.
Think of an OCT image like a vertical slice through the retina. Instead of seeing the surface of the eye, you are seeing layered tissue structure.
NORMAL
A normal macular OCT usually has organized retinal layers, a preserved foveal contour, and no obvious retinal fluid or edema. For this project, NORMAL is the “no visible pathology from the dataset labels” class.

CNV: Choroidal Neovascularization
CNV means abnormal new blood vessel growth from the choroid. It is often associated with neovascular, or “wet,” age-related macular degeneration. In OCT images, CNV may show abnormal membranes, subretinal fluid, or disruption near the retinal pigment epithelium. The OCT2017 dataset descriptions commonly show CNV with neovascular membranes and associated subretinal fluid.

DME: Diabetic Macular Edema
DME is swelling of the macula caused by diabetic retinal microvascular damage. The AAO describes DME as involving hard exudates and edema in the macula secondary to retinal microvascular damage.
In OCT, DME often appears as retinal thickening and dark cyst-like spaces caused by intraretinal fluid.

DRUSEN
Drusen are deposits associated with age-related macular degeneration. In OCT, they often appear as elevations or irregularities around the retinal pigment epithelium.
